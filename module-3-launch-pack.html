The 5-Day
Launch Calendar

Most people treat launching like a single moment. It's not. It's a sequence. Each day has one job. Do the job. Move to the next day. Fill in your dates before you do anything else.

Day 1 Set Up Your Selling Page
Date

Your one job today: get your product live somewhere people can buy it. No content. No posting. Just a live page with a buy button.

What your selling page needs and nothing else:

  • A headline that states the result — not your product name, the result
  • 3–5 bullet points describing what's inside — each starting with a verb
  • A price — pick one and commit, you can change it later
  • A buy button connected to a real payment method

No long story. No guarantee section. No FAQ. Those come in version two.

Bad headline"The Launch Kit"
Good headline"Everything You Need To Launch Your First Digital Product This Week"

Where to set this up:

Pick one. Do not switch halfway through. Get the page live before you go to bed tonight.

Day 2 Soft Launch To 10 People
Date

Before you post publicly you tell 10 real people directly. Not followers. Ten humans who know you and would genuinely care about or benefit from what you built. This is not spam. This is a personal message to 10 specific people.

Write the names before you send anything:

    The Soft Launch Message

    Adapt this to sound like you. Do not copy it word for word.

    "Hey [name]. I just finished building something I think you'd actually find useful. It's called [product name] and it helps [one sentence description]. I'm launching it this week at [price] and you're one of the first people I'm telling. Here's the link if you want to take a look: [your URL]. No pressure at all.     Day 3 Write Your Launch Post
    Date

    Today you write the post that goes public tomorrow. Not posting it today. Writing it, reading it back, and making sure it sounds like a human wrote it. Five parts. One to three sentences each maximum.

    Build Your Launch Post
    Part 1 The Hook

    The first line. It is the only thing people see before they decide whether to keep reading. States a surprising fact, calls out a specific person and their problem, or makes a bold statement that creates curiosity.

    Part 2 The Problem

    2–3 sentences describing the exact situation your buyer is in right now. If they read this and think "that's exactly me" — you've written it correctly.

    Part 3 What You Built and Why

    1–2 sentences. What the product is, what it does, and why you built it. Keep it simple.

    Part 4 What They Get

    3–5 things. Write them as outcomes not features. "You'll have a live selling page by day one" beats "includes a setup guide" every time.

    Part 5 The Call To Action

    One sentence. Tell them exactly what to do. Be direct.

    Day 4 Go Public
    Date

    Today you post your launch post publicly. One platform. The one where your people already are. Make one adjustment if Day 2 feedback gave you something obvious to fix. One. Not a full rewrite.

    After you post, do these three things in this order:

    • Reply to every comment within the first two hours
    • Share the post to your stories or repost it with one added line of context
    • Send the link directly to five more people not on your Day 2 list

    Part 2 of 3

    Selling Page
    Copy Template

    Fill in the six blanks and your selling page is done. Do not add more sections. Do not write an essay. Fill in the blanks and publish.

    Blank 1 of 6
    Your Headline

    State the result. Not the product name. The result.

    Formula: "[Specific result] in [specific timeframe] even if [main objection]"

    Example"Launch Your First Digital Product This Week Even If You Have No Audience and No Idea Where To Start"
    Blank 2 of 6
    What This Is

    One to two sentences describing exactly what the product is and what format it's in.

    Example"The Launch Kit is a plug-and-play system that gives you every template, prompt, and step-by-step guide you need to go from idea to first sale in five days."
    Blank 3 of 6
    This Is For You If

    3–5 specific descriptions of your buyer and their situation. Each one starting with "You".

    ExampleYou have an idea you've been sitting on for months and haven't done anything with it.
    You've tried to start before but got stuck on what to build or how to sell it.
    You want to make money online but don't want to spend six months learning first.
    Blank 4 of 6
    What You Get

    List every item inside the product as an outcome not a feature.

    ExampleThe Idea Filter so you know exactly which idea to build first.
    The MVV Builder so you build only what your product actually needs.
    15 launch post templates so you never stare at a blank screen.
    Blank 5 of 6
    Price and Access

    Your price and what happens after they buy.

    Example"$47. You get instant access to everything. No waiting. No upsell required to get the full product."
    Blank 6 of 6
    Closing Line

    One final line that removes hesitation.

    Example"Everything you need to launch is already inside. The only thing left is to start."

    The Soft Launch
    DM Script

    Use this word for word or adapt it to your voice. Works for Instagram, Threads, WhatsApp, or any direct message platform.

    Message 1 of 4 The first contact
    "Hey [name]. Quick one. I just finished building [product name] and you're one of the first people I'm telling about it. It's a [format] that helps [specific person] [specific result]. Thought of you because [one specific reason relevant to them]. Here's the link if you want a look: [URL]"
    Message 2 of 4 If they open but don't reply within 48 hours
    "Hey [name]. Just checking you got my last message okay. No pressure at all. Genuinely just wanted to make sure it landed. Happy to answer any questions if you have them."
    Message 3 of 4 If they ask what it is
    "It's a [format] that walks you through [main process or outcome] step by step. Includes [most valuable element], [second most valuable element], and [third most valuable element]. The whole thing is designed to get you [result] in [timeframe] without [main obstacle]. It's [price] and you get everything instantly. Want me to send you the link?"
    Message 4 of 4 If they're interested but haven't bought
    "Totally get it. If it helps, the price goes up after [date or number of buyers]. But genuinely no pressure. If it's useful to you it'll still be here. Just let me know if you have any questions."
