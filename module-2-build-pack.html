What Are You
Actually Building?

Before you touch any tool or template, pick the format. Most people overcomplicate this. There are really only a handful of formats that work for a first product. Pick the one that fits. If more than one fits, pick the simplest one.

PDF Guide
5–30 pages. Teaches, explains, or walks someone through something. Google Docs exports to PDF for free.
Best for: knowledge, processes, frameworks, how-to, checklists
Notion Template
A pre-built workspace someone duplicates and uses immediately. No heavy writing required.
Best for: systems, planners, trackers, dashboards, workflows
Canva Template
Pre-designed file someone opens, swaps text and colors, and uses or sells. Build once, sell forever.
Best for: social templates, slide decks, lead magnets, brand kits
Swipe File / Prompt Pack
A collection of copy, scripts, or prompts someone uses directly. No design needed. A Google Doc works fine.
Best for: email sequences, captions, scripts, AI prompts, DM templates
Mini Email Course
5–7 emails that teach one specific thing. Written once, delivered automatically forever.
Best for: education, skill building, step-by-step progression

The MVV Builder

MVV = Minimum Viable Version. The smallest real version of your product that someone can actually use and benefit from. Not a placeholder. Not a draft. A real thing with real value.

      The "What To
      Actually Make"
      Decision Tree

      Use this if you're still unsure what your product should look like or contain.

      Can you solve their problem by giving them information?
      Yes →PDF guide, swipe file, or prompt pack. See the PDF path below.
      No →Keep reading the questions below.
      Can you solve it by giving them a system or template?
      Yes →Notion template, Canva template, or worksheet pack. See the template path below.
      No →Keep reading.
      Can you solve it by teaching them something step by step?
      Yes →Mini email course or written guide with exercises. See the course path below.
      No →Your problem might be too vague. Go back to Module 1 and tighten your offer.
      Path A The PDF Path
      • A clear title that states the result, not the topic
      • An intro that tells them exactly what they'll have by the end
      • 3–7 sections, each solving one specific part of the problem
      • At least one actionable exercise or fill-in per section
      • A final section that tells them what to do next
      • 8–20 pages total. Do not pad it. Longer is not more valuable.
      Tool: Google Docs → Export as PDF
      Path B The Template Path
      • A clear instruction page at the top explaining how to use it
      • Pre-filled examples so they see what done looks like
      • Clear labels on every section — nothing should be confusing
      • Empty versions of each section for their own content
      • A "Start Here" note that tells them step one immediately
      Tool: Notion for systems · Canva for visual templates
      Path C The Course Path
      • A welcome email that sets expectations and previews what's coming
      • One lesson per day, covering exactly one concept or action
      • Each lesson ending with one task they complete before the next
      • A final lesson that celebrates their progress and tells them the next step
      • 5–7 emails total. 300–500 words each. Short enough to read in 5 minutes.
      Tool: Whatever email tool you already have

      The 3 AI Prompts

      Copy and paste these directly into ChatGPT or Claude. Fill in the brackets with your actual information before you hit send.

      Prompt 1 of 3
      Build Your Product Outline
      "I'm creating a [format: PDF guide / swipe file / Notion template / email course] for [your specific person] that helps them [your specific result]. Here is my offer in one sentence: [paste your offer sentence from Module 1].

Give me a complete outline for this product. Include a title, a subtitle, all sections or modules with a one sentence description of each, and the key action or takeaway from each section. The product should be simple enough to build in one week and valuable enough that someone would pay $47 to $97 for it. No filler sections. Only what someone actually needs to get the result."

      Prompt 2 of 3
      Write Your Product Introduction
      "I'm writing the introduction for a [format] called [your working title]. It's for [your specific person] who struggles with [their specific problem]. By the end of the product they will [specific result].

Write an introduction for this product that does four things: tells them exactly what they will have by the end, makes them feel understood without being dramatic about it, explains why this approach works when others haven't, and tells them exactly how to use the product. Keep it under 300 words. Plain and simple language. No motivational speaker energy."
      Paste your results here
      Prompt 3 of 3
      Write One Complete Section
      "I'm writing a section called [section name] for my product [product title]. This section needs to help [your specific person] do [specific action or understand specific concept].

Write this section in full. Include a plain language explanation of the concept, a real example of what this looks like in practice, and one specific action the reader takes at the end of this section before moving on. Keep the explanation under 250 words. The action should be completable in under 20 minutes."
