Part 3 of 4

The Micro-Win
Log

One sentence per day recording one concrete thing you did. That's it. It shows you that you are making progress even on days that feel like nothing happened. And it builds evidence that you are someone who shows up. That evidence matters more than any motivational content you will ever consume.

How to use it: Every day after your work session write one sentence. Not a journal entry. Not a reflection. One sentence recording one concrete action.

The 4 AI Prompts

Copy and paste these directly into ChatGPT or Claude. Fill in the brackets with your actual information before you hit send.

Prompt 1 of 4
Generate 30 Days of Post Ideas
"I just launched a product called [product name]. It is a [format] that helps [specific person] [specific result]. My audience is mostly on [platform] and they are typically [describe your audience in two to three sentences including their main problem, their level of experience, and what they care about].

Give me 30 specific post ideas for the next 30 days. Organize them into four weeks with a clear theme for each week. Each idea should be one sentence describing exactly what the post is about. No generic ideas. Every single one should be specific to my product, my audience, and my topic. Include a mix of value posts, behind the scenes posts, direct sales posts, and trust building posts."

Prompt 2 of 4
Write Your Objection Handler
"I sell [product name] at [price] to [specific person]. The most common objection I hear is [paste the exact objection in the words your buyer uses].

Write me three different responses to this objection. Each response should be conversational, not salesy. Each one should acknowledge the objection genuinely before reframing it. One response should be for a DM conversation, one for a comment reply, and one for a social media post that addresses the objection publicly. Keep each response under 100 words."

Prompt 3 of 4
Turn a Buyer Result Into a Post
"A customer just told me [paste their exact words or a description of their result]. They bought [product name] and this is what happened for them.

Turn this into three different social media posts. One that leads with their result, one that leads with the problem they had before buying, and one that leads with a question my audience can see themselves in. Keep each post under 150 words. Do not make it sound like an ad. Make it sound like a real person sharing a real story."
